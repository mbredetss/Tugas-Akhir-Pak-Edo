Workout nutrition explained. What to eat before, during, and after exercise.

  • by Admin
  • Aug,15, 2019
  • 20 Comment

Nutrition plays an important role in your exercise performance, muscle recovery, and achieving fitness goals. What you eat before, during, and after exercise can significantly impact your workout results. This article will provide a practical guide to sports nutrition, explaining what you need to consume in each phase of training to maximize results and maintain a healthy body.

Food intake before exercise is very important to provide the energy the body needs during exercise. Ideally, consume foods rich in complex carbohydrates and a little protein 1-2 hours before exercise. For example, you can choose brown rice with grilled chicken or whole wheat toast with eggs.

During exercise, especially for high-intensity or long-duration training sessions, it is important to maintain fluid and energy intake. Drink water regularly to avoid dehydration. You can also consume sports drinks that contain electrolytes.

The post-exercise phase is a crucial period for muscle recovery and growth. Consume foods rich in protein and complex carbohydrates within 30 minutes to 2 hours after exercise. This will help repair muscle tissue damaged during exercise and support new muscle growth. Examples of foods that are suitable for consumption after exercise are chicken with brown rice and vegetables, or a protein smoothie with fruit and vegetables.

Although calories are important in exercise, focus on eating healthy, nutritious foods. Choose foods rich in protein, complex carbohydrates, healthy fats, vitamins and minerals to support sports performance and overall health. Limit your intake of processed foods, sugar and saturated fats.

Sports nutrition needs are individual. Experiment with different types of food before, during, and after exercise to find the diet that works best for you. Pay attention to your body's response and adjust your nutritional intake based on training results and body condition. By paying attention to these guidelines and consulting with a sports nutritionist if necessary, you can optimize sports nutrition to support achieving your fitness goals and maintaining a healthy body.
